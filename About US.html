Smart City Mission

The Smart Cities Mission is a Centrally Sponsored Program under the Ministry of Urban Development Government of India. Under this Mission, the Government of India plans to support 100 cities with an outlay of Rs. 50,000 crore (or Rs. 500 crore per city) with a matching grant from State Governments/Urban Local Bodies. Its duration will be five years from 2015-16 to 2019-20. The support under this Mission is envisaged through a Smart Cities Challenge comprising two rounds. Stage I challenge at the State level has completed, following which 12 cities from Tamil Nadu have been selected Erode city is one of the cities selected from Tamil Nadu following the Stage I challenge. The shortlisted cities from all States would now participate in a State II challenge in which each of the selected cities will prepare and submit a Smart City Proposal (SCP) to compete with each other at the National level. 20 cities that fare best in this competition would be selected for funding during 2015-16. The other cities will get a chance to improve their proposals and be eligible to participate in challenge round during the next financial year. Erode corporation consecutively participated next two rounds but Erode didn’t selected , Erode Corporation participating Round 4.

Objectives and Approach:

The objective is to promote cities that provide core infrastructure and give a decent quality of life to its citizens, a clean and sustainable environment and application of ‘Smart’ Solutions. The focus is on sustainable and inclusive development and the idea is to look at compact areas, create a replicable model for other aspiring cities. The Smart Cities Mission of the Government is a bold, new initiative. It is meant to set examples that can be replicated both within and outside the Smart City, catalysing the creation of similar Smart Cities in various regions and parts of the country.

The core infrastructure elements in a Smart City included:
  1. Adequate water supply
  2. Assured electricity supply
  3. Sanitation, including solid waste management
  4. Efficient urban mobility and public transport
  5. Affordable housing, especially for the poor
  6. Robust IT connectivity and digitalization
  7. Good governance, especially e-Governance and citizen participation
  8. Sustainable environment
  9. Safety and security of citizens, particularly women, children and the elderly
  10. Health and education
Strategic Goals:

SG 1. ECO-FRIENDLY City with Improved health parameters.
  • Sub Goal (A) : Pollution Free City (Water/ Air) by canal rejuvenation and making city pollution proof
  • Sub Goal (B) : Enhanced Green Cover in city by creating open spaces
  • Sub Goal (C) : Efficient and Effective Health facilities
  • Sub Goal (D) : Increase share of Clean Energy
SG 2. To become "INCLUSIVE AND VIBRANT ECONOMY" leveraging intrinsic strength of being a business destination for Agro based and Textiles Industrial hub.
  • Sub Goal (A) : Modernisation of Textile Industry
  • Sub Goal (B) : Industrial Infrastructure to boost the economy
SG 3. A "LIVEABLE CITY" with BEST-IN-CLASS URBAN SERVICES DELIVERY.
  • Sub Goal (A) : Improved Urban service delivery including
    1. Strengthening of Water (100% access) and Waste Water reuse
    2. 100% Storm Water Coverage
    3. 100% Solid Waste Management (Integrated) and Sanitation services under SBM
    4. Efficient Street lighting System
    5. Strengthening of Power distribution system
  • Sub Goal (B) : SMART Education in Schools
  • Sub Goal (C) : Housing for Urban Poor
SG 4. To provide "SAFE AND EFFICIENT TRANSIT SYSTEM" to its citizen.
  • Sub Goal (A) : Reduce Accidents and Fatalities to Zero
  • Sub Goal (B) : Increase share of Public Transport
  • Sub Goal (C) : Improve NMT Facilities and reduce Traffic Congestion
SG 5. City with "TRANSPARENT AND EFFICIENT GOVERNANCE"involving citizen participation.
  • Sub Goal (A) : Transparent Seamless Governance and Effective Citizen Engagement
  • Sub Goal (B) : Rationalise Taxes and Fees to make SCP financially sustainable

A) Area Based Proposal

A comprehensive and replicable RETROFIT development of southern Erode along the Perumpallam Canal (2251 acres) is envisaged under ABD area development. The selected ABD Area is shown below.

To enhance urban quality of living and improved urban environment in ABD area, the actions along the following four themes are proposed.
  • REVIVAL AND RESTORATION OF THE 12 km PERUMPALLAM WATER FRONT to pristine water quality and health, while reinforcing its flood handling capacity and creating life-spaces (with natural habitats, recreational, educational and aquatic features)
  • IMPROVED MOBILITY through a re-configured street network, linking it with the water front through modern NMT pathways (with solar panelling, energy efficient lighting, dedicated cycle tracks), and linkages to transport hubs (bus terminal and railway station)
  • CREATION of a SLUM-FREE neighbourhood through provision of affordable housing and social infrastructure for the 1200 low-income households living in the area.
  • UNIVERSAL ACCESS to SMART URBAN SERVICES involving comprehensive retrofit of physical and social infrastructure, embedded with Smart solutions.
